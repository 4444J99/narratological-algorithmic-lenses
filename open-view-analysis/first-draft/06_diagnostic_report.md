**Summary Statement:**

*Open View* is an ambitious script with a strong formal concept (quadrant overture, surveillance grammar) and compelling themes (family as already dead, gaze as violence). However, it suffers from a critically incomplete third act, weak causal logic between the family's dysfunction and the "game," and an underdeveloped HQ subplot that consumes pages without payoff. The first draft establishes a world and tone effectively but fails to dramatize its climax and resolution.

---

## II. Critical Structural Issues

### CRITICAL ISSUE C1: Incomplete Third Act

**Severity:** ●●●●● (Script-Breaking)

**Description:**
The script terminates at line 2810, mid-scene. The "moral tests" for Sus and Rok are sketched in narrative notes (line 2717: "They make them turn on each other...") rather than dramatized scenes. Li's collaborator reveal gets two lines. Fre's fate after being drugged is undescribed. The Black Box's ultimate purpose remains unexplained.

**Evidence:**
- Lines 2717: "They make them turn on each other, to fulfill a desire. But whoever decides to turn first is turned against them and they are the one killed." — This is a narrative note, not a scene.
- Lines 2784-2793: Fre is "tricked" and "drugged" but the scene is described, not written.
- Lines 2800-2810: Final beats are plot summary, not screenplay pages.

**Impact:**
Without a complete third act, the script cannot be evaluated for climax effectiveness, thematic resolution, or emotional payoff. The current "ending" is a stopping point, not an ending.

**Effort Required:** HIGH (15-25 pages of new material)

---

### CRITICAL ISSUE C2: HQ Subplot Integration Failure

**Severity:** ●●●●○ (Major)

**Description:**
The HQ subplot (Wot, Jra, Lon) consumes approximately 300 lines of screenplay but never integrates with the family's story. We never understand why HQ chose this family, what the "game" accomplishes, or what Lon's rebellion achieves. The subplot creates parallel action without meaningful connection.

**Evidence:**
- Lines 945-958: HQ Control Room introduced with silhouetted figures. No explanation of purpose.
- Lines 1372-1407: Wot and Jra discuss "Series A vs Series B" selection criteria. Never explained.
- Lines 1623-1670: Lon gathers intelligence, replays footage. To what end?
- Lines 2794-2799: Lon "tricks Wot" and "reveals whole company." Narrative note, not scene.

**Impact:**
The HQ subplot suggests a larger world but delivers nothing. It raises questions it doesn't answer and promises rebellion it doesn't dramatize. Either the subplot needs full development (explaining the system, dramatizing the rebellion) or it should be cut to preserve mystery.

**Effort Required:** HIGH (if developing) / MEDIUM (if cutting)

---

### CRITICAL ISSUE C3: Weak Causal Logic

**Severity:** ●●●●○ (Major)

**Description:**
The Black Box "activates" at TIMECODE 00:00:00, but nothing the family does triggers this event. The game appears arbitrary—imposed externally with no causal connection to character action. This weakens dramatic logic: in strong structure, catastrophe flows from character choice.

**Evidence:**
- Lines 593-668: Black Box dropped by drone. Family brings it inside casually.
- Lines 2016-2034: Lef's crisis begins (alarm, temperature rise). No trigger identified.
- Line 2415: "We force families apart that should never be put back together again." But why *this* family? Why *tonight*?

**Impact:**
The arbitrary activation creates structural gap. The family's dysfunction is established, but the connection between their sins and their punishment is never drawn. Even a single line explaining the selection criteria would help.

**Effort Required:** LOW (one scene or dialogue addition)

## III. High Priority Issues

### ISSUE H1: Li's Collaborator Reveal Underdeveloped

**Severity:** ●●●○○ (Important)

**Description:**
Li's reveal as HQ collaborator at line 2806 is the script's major twist, but it receives only two lines: "Li starts to panic. She reaches out to the camera revealing herself to be in on the whole plan." No motivation, no confrontation, no consequences.

**Location:** Lines 2804-2810

**Recommendation:**
- Seed earlier hints of Li's collaboration (her "wave of dread" at Lef's acceptance letters; her calm during the Black Box arrival; her violence as performance)
- Dramatize the reveal: family confronts Li; Li justifies or confesses
- Show consequences: does she survive? does she escape?

**Effort:** MEDIUM (develop existing beats + add scenes)

---

### ISSUE H2: Sus/Mis Sar Subplot Abandoned

**Severity:** ●●●○○ (Important)

**Description:**
The sexual abuse subplot is introduced with disturbing specificity (Mis Sar's "stretching" session) then dropped until the "test" where Mis Sar appears naked in Sus's shower. The abuse is never confronted, and the "test" outcome ("Mis Sar isn't really there?") is unclear.

**Location:** Lines 1172-1248 (abuse), Lines 2684-2782 (test)

**Recommendation:**
- Clarify whether Mis Sar in the shower is hologram, hallucination, or real
- Show consequences of Sus's "revenge" choice
- Connect abuse thread to family's complicity (did parents know?)

**Effort:** MEDIUM

---

### ISSUE H3: Voice Indistinction (HQ Characters)

**Severity:** ●●○○○ (Moderate)

**Description:**
Wot, Jra, and Lon speak in nearly identical abstract registers. Their dialogue is oracular but interchangeable. Without distinctive voices, they blur into a single "system" presence.

**Location:** All HQ scenes

**Recommendation:**
If retaining HQ subplot:
- Wot: older, more formal, confident
- Jra: questioning, uncertain, morally conflicted
- Lon: terse, action-oriented, subversive

**Effort:** LOW (dialogue polish)

---

### ISSUE H4: Rok's Death Brief

**Severity:** ●●○○○ (Moderate)

**Description:**
Rok's drowning with Gre—the script's most emotionally charged beat—receives only 7 lines (2770-2777). After extensive setup of Rok's suppressed desire, the payoff is rushed.

**Location:** Lines 2756-2782

**Recommendation:**
- Extend the drowning sequence
- Show Rok's recognition that desire = death
- Show Gre's reaction (does he understand? accept? resist?)
- Show parents watching (Li's reaction; Fre's reaction)

**Effort:** LOW-MEDIUM (extend existing scene)

## VII. Causal Binding Test

**Method:** Between each major beat, insert either BUT, THEREFORE, or AND THEN. BUT and THEREFORE indicate strong causal binding. AND THEN indicates weak/episodic connection.

| Beat Transition | Natural Connector | Assessment |
|-----------------|-------------------|------------|
| Prologue → Divorce | THEREFORE (loops establish dysfunction) | ✓ |
| Divorce → Breakfast | AND THEN (scenes parallel, not causal) | ✗ |
| Black Box Arrival → Family Dispersal | AND THEN (box sits inert; family ignores) | ✗ |
| Parallel Betrayals → Convergence | AND THEN (time-based, not causal) | ✗ |
| Lef Crisis → Family Trapped | THEREFORE (alarm triggers lockdown) | ✓ |
| Lef's Death → Tests | THEREFORE (death triggers test phase) | ✓ |
| Rok's Test → Li's Reveal | AND THEN (weak connection) | ✗ |

**Causal Binding Score:** ~40% (weak)

**Assessment:** The script's parallel structure creates many AND THEN connections. Movement III especially lacks causal binding—each family member's day proceeds independently. The siege section (Movement IV-V) has stronger causation but still lacks the trigger that connects family behavior to game activation.
