## I. Market Segment Analysis

### Primary Market: Festival-to-Streaming Art Horror

*Open View* fits the festival-circuit-to-premium-streaming pipeline that has characterized art horror successes over the past decade.

**Market Characteristics:**
- Prestige festival premiere builds critical discourse
- Limited theatrical capitalizes on festival buzz
- Streaming provides long-tail revenue
- Audience: educated, urban, 25-45, film-literate

## III. Positioning Challenges

### Challenge 1: Genre Confusion

**Problem:** *Open View* defies easy genre categorization.
- Is it domestic drama?
- Is it surveillance thriller?
- Is it body horror?
- Is it reality TV satire?

**Marketing Implication:** Trailer and poster must establish tone; audiences need category expectations managed.

**Comparable Approach:** *mother!* was marketed as psychological horror; audiences expecting straightforward genre were alienated. *Open View* should either:
- Lean into ambiguity (art cinema positioning)
- Establish clear genre expectation (horror positioning)

---

### Challenge 2: Tonal Whiplash

**Problem:** The script shifts from Cassavetes-style marital drama to body horror to meditation.

**Marketing Implication:** Must prepare audiences for tonal journey without spoiling surprises.

**Comparable Approach:** *Parasite* shifted from comedy to thriller to tragedy; marketing emphasized "genre-defying" as positive attribute.

---

### Challenge 3: Extreme Content

**Problem:** Child death; boiling; violence; sexual content.

**Marketing Implication:** Limits advertising platforms; requires MPAA negotiation; may face international censorship.

**Comparable Approach:** *Raw* (cannibalism) and *Titane* (extreme body horror) found audiences despite—or because of—extreme content. Festival-circuit audiences expect provocation.

---

### Challenge 4: Incomplete Narrative

**Problem:** Current draft is structurally incomplete.

**Marketing Implication:** Cannot market or position until third act is written.

**Resolution Required:** Complete script before production attachment.

## IV. Marketing Strategy Recommendations

### Strategy A: Art Cinema Positioning

**Target Audience:** Film-literate viewers; festival attendees; art-house regulars
**Tone:** Provocative; intellectual; disturbing
**Comparisons:** "From the tradition of Haneke and Fassbinder"
**Visual Identity:** Quadrant imagery; surveillance aesthetic; clinical
**Festival Target:** Venice, Toronto, Cannes (Un Certain Regard)

**Advantages:**
- Authentic to script's ambitions
- Critical discourse generates word-of-mouth
- Lower marketing spend required

**Risks:**
- Limited audience ceiling
- May not recoup budget without streaming sale

---

### Strategy B: Horror Positioning

**Target Audience:** Horror genre fans; 18-35 demographic
**Tone:** Terrifying; transgressive; don't-look-away
**Comparisons:** "If *Funny Games* met *Black Mirror*"
**Visual Identity:** Grow tank; boiling child imagery; family in peril
**Festival Target:** Sundance Midnight, SXSW Midnighters, Fantastic Fest

**Advantages:**
- Larger potential audience
- Horror fans seek extreme content
- Genre community drives opening weekend

**Risks:**
- Genre expectations may clash with art cinema pacing
- Could alienate both audiences if balance is wrong

---

### Strategy C: Hybrid/Elevated Horror

**Target Audience:** "Elevated horror" fans; crossover audience
**Tone:** Smart horror; disturbing ideas; social commentary
**Comparisons:** "The next *Get Out* meets *The Platform*"
**Visual Identity:** Suburban glass house; family dysfunction; surveillance motifs
**Festival Target:** Sundance, Toronto (Platform or Midnight)

**Advantages:**
- Largest potential audience
- Positions with successful recent comparables
- Appeals to both cinephiles and genre fans

**Risks:**
- "Elevated horror" fatigue in marketplace
- Must deliver on both art and horror promises

**Recommendation:** Strategy C (Hybrid positioning) offers best risk/reward balance, but execution must satisfy both audience segments.

## V. Distribution Recommendations

### Optimal Path

1. **Premiere:** Venice Film Festival (Competition or Horizons) or Toronto International Film Festival (Platform)

2. **Sales:** International sales at festival; U.S. distribution (A24, NEON, or Focus Features)

3. **Theatrical:** Limited platform release (10-25 screens); expand based on performance

4. **Streaming:** Premium streaming after theatrical window (MUBI, Shudder, or major platform acquisition)

### Alternative Path

1. **Skip Festival:** If festival premiere not secured

2. **Streaming Sale:** Pre-production or post-production sale to Netflix/Shudder/MUBI

3. **Day-and-Date:** Limited theatrical + streaming simultaneous release

## VI. Audience Research Questions

Before final marketing strategy, research should address:

1. **Genre Expectations:** How do test audiences describe the film after viewing? Does their genre categorization align with marketing intention?

2. **Tone Tolerance:** At what point (if any) do test audiences disengage? Is it during parallel section pacing or extreme content?

3. **Ending Response:** (Requires completed script) Does the resolution satisfy or frustrate? Is frustration intentional and effective?

4. **Comparison Resonance:** Which comparisons generate most interest? *Funny Games*? *Black Mirror*? *Get Out*?

5. **Content Sensitivity:** Does Lef's death sequence alienate or intensify engagement? Different regions may respond differently.

## VIII. Final Market Assessment

### Viability: CONDITIONAL PROCEED

*Open View* has market potential in the art horror segment, but current state (incomplete first draft) makes assessment provisional.

**Proceed Contingent On:**
1. Third act completion and script lock
2. Director attachment with festival credibility
3. Budget in $5-10M range
4. Financing structure including streaming presale or gap financing
5. Marketing strategy decided pre-production

**Not Recommended:**
- Wide theatrical release (audience too narrow)
- Budget exceeding $15M (return unlikely)
- Production without completed script
- Marketing without clear genre positioning

### Comparable Success Model

*Open View* should target the *Hereditary* model:
- Festival premiere builds buzz
- Limited theatrical opens to engaged audience
- Word-of-mouth expands platform
- Streaming provides long-tail revenue
- Total return of 3-5x budget

Achieving this requires:
- Complete, polished script
- Director with festival track record
- Cast with genre credibility
- Execution that satisfies art and horror audiences
