## III. Dialogue Pattern Analysis

### Strong Patterns (Preserve)

1. **Marital Combat:** Li and Fre's exchanges in the bathroom scene demonstrate authentic conflict—insults that contain truth, cruelty that reveals history, flashes of shared language ("You used to be funny").

2. **Degradation Dynamic:** Fre and Nol's exchanges use humiliation as intimacy. "You're a pig" / "Shut up, cunt" establishes their relationship instantly.

3. **Thesis Statement:** "Splayed, weighed, and displayed" is strong, memorable, and thematically precise.

### Weak Patterns (Address)

1. **HQ Abstraction:** All HQ dialogue is vague. "Was Series B the right one?" / "It's going to be one of those?" / "This has been one huge mistake" — none of this is grounded.

2. **Youth Generic:** Sus and Rok sound like any teens. No slang, no generational markers, no distinctive idiom.

3. **Narrative Notes as Dialogue:** Many late scenes describe dialogue rather than writing it. "Fre is tricked into thinking..." / "Li starts to panic..."

## V. Subtext Analysis

### High Subtext Scenes

1. **Bathroom Argument (411-560):** Every line carries double meaning. "You got cold" = she withdrew emotionally AND she became calculating. "You got lazy" = he stopped trying AND he gave up on himself.

2. **Fre Begs Nol (1544-1620):** "Runaway with me" contains: save me from my life; validate my delusions; prove I'm lovable. Her rejection ("Your cloud is going to follow you") is both literal (you can't escape) and metaphorical (your psychology follows you).

3. **Li's Violence (1491-1527):** The dialogue is minimal, but "Meet again next week?" before violence contains: we both know this is transactional; my capacity for harm is showing.

### Low Subtext Scenes

1. **All HQ Scenes:** Characters say exactly what they mean in abstract terms. "This has been one huge mistake" has no layers—it's just a statement.

2. **Black Box Tests:** "An opportunity presents itself" is clear and functional but lacks the complexity of the thesis statement.

3. **Breakfast Bickering (669-768):** Functional exposition but no additional layer. Characters talk about what they're talking about.

## VII. Recommendations

### Tier 1: Critical

1. **Write the missing dialogue.** Act III is narrative notes. The test scenes, Li's reveal, Fre's manipulation, and resolution all need actual dialogue.

2. **Distinguish HQ voices (if keeping subplot).** Wot: formal, confident. Jra: questioning, uncertain. Lon: terse, action-oriented.

### Tier 2: Important

3. **Develop Sus's voice.** Give her one scene with sustained dialogue—perhaps her "test" confrontation with Mis Sar.

4. **Add Rok/Gre dialogue.** The drowning scene needs words, even if fragmentary.

5. **Refine test announcements.** Echo the thesis ("splayed, weighed, displayed") in the test language.

### Tier 3: Polish

6. **Tighten bathroom argument.** Could cut 20-30 lines while preserving rhythm.

7. **Add youth markers.** Give Sus and Rok contemporary speech patterns.

8. **Ensure Li's lines seed collaboration.** Add moments of emotional distance that retrospectively reveal her complicity.
